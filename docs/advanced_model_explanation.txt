================================================================================
                         COMPOSITE ALPHA MODEL
                     Architecture & Design Rationale
================================================================================

1. OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Composite Alpha is an advanced paper-trading strategy that fuses four
microstructure and stochastic sub-signals into a single directional score,
gates every entry on a multi-factor confidence check, and wraps every
position in an asymmetric stop-loss / take-profit bracket where the
take-profit is always wider than the stop-loss (default 2.5×).

Design philosophy: quality over quantity.
The model trades infrequently (minimum 30 s between trades) and only when
multiple independent signals agree and market conditions are favourable.


2. SUB-SIGNALS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Each sub-signal is normalised to the range [-1, +1].

2.1  LOB Queue Imbalance  (weight 0.25)
     Source: Cont & de Larrard, "Price Dynamics in a Markovian Limit Order
             Market", SIAM J. Financial Math, 2013.

     imbalance = best_bid_qty / (best_bid_qty + best_ask_qty)

     If imbalance > 0.60 the bid queue is thicker than the ask queue.
     This means the ask side is more likely to be consumed first, pushing
     the price upward.  The signal scales linearly from 0 at 0.60 to +1
     at 1.0 (and symmetrically for the short side below 0.40).

2.2  Order Flow Imbalance – OFI  (weight 0.30)
     Source: Cont, Kukanov & Stoikov, "The Price Impact of Order Book
             Events", J. Financial Econometrics, 2014.

     OFI measures event-by-event changes at the best bid and ask:
       • If the best bid price rises or bid size grows  → positive OFI
       • If the best ask price drops or ask size grows  → negative OFI

     The raw OFI is accumulated over a rolling 50-event window and
     normalised by its empirical standard deviation to produce a z-score.
     A positive z-score means sustained buy pressure; negative means
     sustained sell pressure.

     OFI receives the highest weight (0.30) because it captures the
     *dynamics* of the order book, not just a snapshot.

2.3  Kyle Flow Momentum  (weight 0.20)
     Source: Kyle, "Continuous Auctions and Insider Trading",
             Econometrica, 1985.

     Kyle's model relates price changes to aggregate order flow through a
     price-impact coefficient lambda:

         ΔP ≈ λ · ΔOrderFlow

     The model estimates lambda online via rolling OLS:

         λ̂ = Cov(ΔP, ΔFlow) / Var(ΔFlow)

     over the last 80 book snapshots.  It then computes the predicted
     price move from the most recent 20-snapshot flow burst:

         predicted_move = λ̂ × recent_net_flow

     and normalises by the current mid-price.  A positive predicted move
     implies informed buying hasn't been fully priced in yet.

2.4  Ornstein-Uhlenbeck Mean Reversion  (weight 0.25)
     The mid-price is modelled as a mean-reverting process:

         dX = θ (μ − X) dt + σ dW

     In discrete time:  X_{t+1} = a + b · X_t + ε
     where  b = exp(−θ Δt)  and  a = μ (1 − b).

     Parameters are estimated via OLS regression of X_{t+1} on X_t over
     the full price buffer (~500 ticks).  The signal is a z-score of the
     deviation of the current price from the estimated long-run mean μ̂,
     scaled by the regression residual standard deviation.

     Positive z-score (price below μ̂) → long; negative → short.
     This sub-signal acts as a contrarian anchor that pulls the composite
     signal back toward equilibrium.


3. COMPOSITE SIGNAL AGGREGATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The four sub-signals are combined with static weights:

    composite = 0.25 × imbalance
              + 0.30 × OFI
              + 0.20 × flow_momentum
              + 0.25 × mean_reversion

The result is clamped to [-1, +1].

The weights were chosen to balance two categories:
  • Trend-following / momentum:  imbalance + OFI + flow  (total 0.75)
  • Mean-reversion:              OU z-score              (total 0.25)

This blends short-term momentum (what's happening right now at the top of
book) with a longer-horizon anchor (where the price "should" be).


4. CONFIDENCE GATING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The model does NOT trade on the composite signal alone.  It also computes
a confidence score:

    confidence = agreement × vol_modifier × jump_filter

4.1  Agreement (0 to 1)
     Fraction of the four sub-signals that agree with the composite
     direction.  For example, if composite > 0 and three of the four
     sub-signals are also positive (> +0.05), agreement = 0.75.

     Requiring high agreement prevents the model from acting on signals
     driven by a single noisy sub-component.

4.2  Volatility Modifier (0.3 to 1.5)
     Ratio of long-run realised variance to short-run realised variance.

       • > 1  →  market is quieter than usual  →  signals are more reliable
                 → modifier amplifies confidence  (up to 1.5×)
       • < 1  →  market is noisier than usual  →  signals are less reliable
                 → modifier dampens confidence  (down to 0.3×)

     Short-run RV uses the last 20 log-returns; long-run RV is the mean
     of all short-run RV values seen (up to 200 samples).

4.3  Jump Filter (0 or 1)
     If the most recent log-return exceeds 3.5 standard deviations from
     the rolling mean, a "jump" is flagged and the filter is set to 0
     for 10 subsequent ticks.  This prevents the model from entering
     during sudden price dislocations where the order book is likely
     in a transient, unreliable state.

An entry is only allowed when:
  • |composite| ≥ entry_threshold    (default 0.35)
  • confidence  ≥ confidence_threshold (default 0.60)

This dual gate is the core mechanism for quality-over-quantity: the
model will often see a signal but decline to trade because confidence
is insufficient.


5. RISK MANAGEMENT – ASYMMETRIC SL / TP
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Every position is bracketed with:

    SL_distance = ATR × atr_sl_mult       (default 1.5)
    TP_distance = SL_distance × risk_reward (default 2.5)

where ATR is a simple rolling mean of |ΔPrice| over the last 100 ticks.

For a LONG entry at price P:
    stop-loss   = P − SL_distance
    take-profit = P + TP_distance  =  P + 2.5 × SL_distance

For a SHORT entry at price P:
    stop-loss   = P + SL_distance
    take-profit = P − TP_distance

This means:
  • The stop-loss is TIGHT (1.5 × ATR)
  • The take-profit is WIDE (3.75 × ATR)
  • Risk-reward ratio is 1 : 2.5

Even if only 30-40% of trades are winners, the strategy can be
profitable because each winner earns 2.5× what each loser costs.

The position is exited immediately when the mid-price crosses either
the SL or TP level.  After any exit (SL or TP), the cooldown timer
resets, preventing immediate re-entry.


6. COOLDOWN & TRADE FREQUENCY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Minimum interval between trades: 30 seconds (configurable via cooldown_ms).

This is 6× longer than the base strategies (5 s).  Combined with the
confidence gate, the model typically produces 5-15× fewer trades than the
simpler strategies, but each trade is backed by stronger evidence.


7. THOUGHT PROCESS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The model maintains a scrolling log of its reasoning:

  • Every 20 book ticks: a full "analysis" dump showing all sub-signal
    values, composite score, confidence, volatility regime, jump status,
    position state, and distance to SL / TP (if in a position).

  • On every entry decision:
    - If skipped due to low confidence → logs reason
    - If skipped due to jump → logs reason
    - If entered → logs direction, entry price, SL, TP, signal, confidence

  • On every exit: logs exit price, whether SL or TP was hit, and
    the per-unit price delta.

This log is displayed in real time on the dashboard, so you can observe
exactly why the model is (or isn't) trading at any moment.


8. PARAMETER SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Parameter                Default    Purpose
─────────────────────    ───────    ──────────────────────────────────
max_position             0.01       Maximum position size (BTC)
cooldown_ms              30,000     Min ms between trades
risk_reward              2.5        TP / SL ratio
atr_sl_mult              1.5        SL = ATR × this
entry_threshold          0.35       Min |composite| to consider entry
confidence_threshold     0.60       Min confidence to allow entry
analysis_every           20         Log full analysis every N ticks

Sub-signal weights:
  imbalance              0.25
  OFI                    0.30
  flow                   0.20
  mean_reversion         0.25


9. LIMITATIONS & FUTURE WORK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
• Weights are static.  An adaptive weighting scheme (e.g. online ridge
  regression of sub-signals against realised returns) could improve
  signal quality.

• The confidence gate is rule-based.  A learned classifier (e.g. small
  neural network predicting trade success from the feature vector) could
  replace or augment it.

• SL/TP levels are fixed at entry.  Trailing stops or dynamic bracket
  adjustment based on post-entry order flow could improve risk management.

• The model does not account for execution costs (spread, slippage, fees).
  In live trading, the TP should be widened to cover round-trip costs.

• Position sizing is binary (max_position or zero).  Kelly-criterion or
  volatility-scaled sizing would be a natural extension.
